Research Priorities

Key bets we are pursuing

Structured reasoning is our main line of inquiry. Self-improving systems and frontier LLM extensions deepen our intuition about what unlocks trustworthy reasoning in current architectures.

Post-training for system-2 reasoning

Design and evaluate procedures that let models plan out chain-of-thought style at inference, including scratchpad planning, tool-augmented thinking, and structured self-reflection that pushes models toward frontier performance.

Self-improving systems

Build recursive training and evaluation loops that let models critique, retrain, and upgrade their own capabilities in stable cycles.

RL-native reasoning agents

Architect reasoning agents around reinforcement-learning paradigms, combining policy learning with tool use, evaluation loops, and dense feedback for rapid improvement.

Multi-agent intelligence

Extend frontier LLM capabilities to coordinated swarms of specialists that share context, route tasks, and search jointly across problem spaces.

Strategy

Progressing towards strong LLMs

We are ruthlessly focused on breakthroughs that compound. Each cycle pushes us toward richer evaluations, self-improving techniques, and the ability to extend current LLM capabilities.

  • Secure runway, frontier agenda

    Independent funding for the next four years lets us push o-style systems and frontier capabilities without distraction.

  • Demonstrate self-improving results

    Run bespoke evaluations to prove that new training and inference loops deliver reliable upgrades in reasoning systems.

  • Scale ambition with evidence

    Success unlocks capital, compute, and talent. We plan to move from GPU-poor to GPU-rich as empirical gains extend LLM capabilities.
